Journey Through Space History

October 4, 1957

Sputnik 1: The Beep That Changed Everything

A 183-pound metal sphere orbiting Earth every 96 minutes sent shockwaves around the world. The Space Age had begun, and humanity would never be the same. The simple radio beeps from Sputnik announced that the impossible had become reality.

April 12, 1961

Gagarin's Giant Leap

"Poyekhali!" (Let's go!) With these words, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to orbit Earth. His 108-minute flight from Launch Pad 1 ‚Äì now forever known as "Gagarin's Start" ‚Äì proved that humans could survive in space and return safely home.

June 16, 1963

Breaking Barriers

Valentina Tereshkova, codenamed "Chaika" (Seagull), became the first woman in space. Her 48 orbits around Earth shattered gender barriers and proved that space exploration belonged to all humanity, not just men.

1971-Present

Space Stations & International Cooperation

From the first Salyut stations to today's International Space Station missions, Baikonur became the launching pad for humanity's permanent presence in space. The Interkosmos program brought together cosmonauts from 13 nations, transforming competition into collaboration.
